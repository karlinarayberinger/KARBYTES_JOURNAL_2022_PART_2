Today while walking around in the bright, hot noon sunshine through the suburban oasis that is Castro Valley, I realized that I am essentially living in my own “parallel universe for one” which feels like traveling through a desert where there is scant shade, little drinkable water, and plenty of hazards. I will not bother talking to other people about my life unless they ask because my life seems to be unrelatable to most others. I imagine that other people would ask me in an exasperated tone, “Why do you spend so much time and money trying to live outside when you have a place to live indoors with plenty of shade, drinking water, bathrooms, and Internet access?” I have a plethora of different reasons why I do my life this way, but I would simply say, “I get too claustrophobic when I spend more than a few hours indoors where other people seem to be watching my every move. Being outside invigorates me and keeps my depression at bay. Some of my best ideas come to me while walking alone through the wilderness. I enjoy such moments more than most others. In fact, my happiest memories are of me spending time alone in the wilderness.”

Thinking of myself as a lone traveler wherever I live prevents me from feeling cemented into the role of home owner and community member (even though I still think of myself as a member of a global progressive community). Unlike seemingly most of the people around me, I have no interest in getting married or in having kids. Therefore, I have little incentive to work my ass off in pursuit of a big house, big car, and other material and cultural trappings which seem to be essential to raising a “modern” family. So I believe that I have plenty of critics who call me a slacker for that reason alone. (That’s to say nothing of my zeal for living in as eco friendly as I can afford and aspiring to be a revolutionary intellectual who focuses on free speech, transhumanism, and analytical philosophy as my primary domains of expertise).

One more thing: I am making an effort to save at least 25% of my monetary income. That seems doable. I am slowly saving up an “emergency fund” without depriving myself of the kind of standard of living I am used to and do not want to have to renounce in order to be more frugal than I currently am. Hence, I do not have much money nor time to spend outside of my rather selfish agenda. I think that is what people seem to nag me the most about: how I seem to only live to please myself and how I seem to be having more fun than I deserve to and how I ought to be making more sacrifices in order to earn their approval. I am not going to waste my time trying to make other people proud of me or even to simply leave me alone. They seem to be addicted to attacking those who do not conform to their conformist agenda.

Well, it’s a beautiful day! Time to keep going.
